# Historical prominent Figure in Finance

Medici banking (1400): Double-entry bookkeeping (italian mathematics, Luca Pacioli, described in a book in 1494). Florence banking house fund Renaissance trade.

First Stock market (1602): The dutch east india company holds the world's first IPO; the Amsterdam Stock Engagement is created 

NYSE Founded (1792): The New York Stock Exchange is established; capital markets formalize in us.

Modern Portfolio Theory (1952): Markowitz's risk return optimization, Finance becomes a mathematical science.

Black-Scholes (1973): The options pricing formula. Derivatives markets explode; financial engineering is born

The CFO role rises (1990): The CFO becomes a strategic partner to the CEO. Finance leads M&A, Capital allocation and investor relations
