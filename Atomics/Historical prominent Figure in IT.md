# Historical prominent Figure in IT

First business computers (1945): ENIAC (1945) and UNIVAC (1951): census data
processing. The first 'IT department' is a room of mathematicians.

MIS emerges (1960): The IBM System/360. Management Information Systems (MIS) are formalized; reports begin to replace intuition.

Personal computer (1975): The Apple Il and IBM PC: computing moves from mainframe rooms to desks. IT democratizes.

ERP revolution (1990): SAP R/3 and Oracle integrate Finance, HR and Operations in a single system. ERP becomes mandatory for large enterprises.

Cloud computing (2004): Salesforce SaaS and AWS (2006): the cloud replaces on-
premises infrastructure. IT shifts from CapEx to OpEx.

Digital transformation (2015): Mobile-first, the API economy, big-data analytics. 'Every company is a technology company' (Satya Nadella).

Generative Al (2023): ChatGPT, Copilot and Gemini change every IT application. The CIO role is fundamentally redefined.

>Trajectory: Data processing Digital architecture for the whole enterprise.
