# Historical evolution of Business Strategies

|**Historical period**|**Dominant form of the function**|**Major conceptual developments**|
|---|---|---|
|Pre-20th century roots|Military strategy, political economy and entrepreneurial judgment|Competition, resource deployment, trade, monopoly, industrial organization and entrepreneurial opportunity.|
|1900s-1950s|Business policy and administrative coordination|General management, planning, budgeting, organization design and coordination of diversified enterprises.|
|1960s-1970s|Long-range planning and portfolio strategy|Corporate planning departments, experience curve, growth-share matrix, PIMS, diversification and capital allocation.|
|1980s|Competitive positioning|Industry structure, five forces, generic strategies, value chain and competitive advantage.|
|1990s|Resource-based and capability-based strategy|Core competence, Resource-Based View (RBV), dynamic capabilities, knowledge-based view and organizational learning.|
|2000s-2010s|Innovation, ecosystems and digital strategy|Blue ocean strategy, platform strategy, ecosystem orchestration, business model innovation and agile strategy.|
|2020s-present|AI-era strategy|AI strategy, data advantage, real-time sensing, strategic resilience, responsible AI, agentic organizations and human-AI operating models.|
