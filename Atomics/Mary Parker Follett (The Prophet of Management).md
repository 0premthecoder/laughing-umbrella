# Mary Parker Follett (The Prophet of Management)

An early management philosopher, social worker, and political scientist. Working in an era dominated by male theorists, she approached organizations as communities rather than machines.

## Management Theory

- **Power "With," Not Power "Over":** Follett argued against coercive power (power _over_ employees) and advocated for co-active power (power _with_ employees). She believed authority should stem from knowledge, function, and expertise rather than formal hierarchical positions.

- **Integrative Conflict Resolution:** She categorized conflict resolution into three types: Domination (win-lose), Compromise (lose-lose, as both parties give something up), and Integration. She strongly advocated for Integration—a creative, win-win solution where the needs of both parties are fully met.

- **Law of the Situation** : The person with the most relevant knowledge should lead in that specific scenario.

- **Reciprocal Relationships:** Follett argued against the one-way, top-down command structure. She proposed "circular response," noting that managers and workers continuously influence and react to one another dynamically.
