# Historical prominent Figure in R&D

Edison's lab (1876): Menlo Park: the first industrial R&D lab. Systematic invention as as organizational activity. 1,093 patents.

Bell Labs (1925): AT&T's Bell Labs invents the transistor, the laser, Unix, the C language and cellular telephony - the gold standard fo" corporate R&D.

DARPA model (1945): US government R&D funds the internet, GPS and
semiconductors. Government as innovation catalyst.

R&D departments spread (1970): Pharma, chemicals and electronics: every major
company builds in-house R&D. The IP portfolio becomes an asset.

Open innovation (2000): Companies source innovation externally. M&A, partnerships and start-ups complement lab-only R&D.

Design thinking (2010): IDEO and Stanford 'd.school' formalize human-centred
innovation: design + technology + business. 

Al-accelerated R&D (2023): AlphaFold solved ~200 million protein structures in two years - a 50-year biology grand challenge. Al compresses R&D timelines significantly.

>Trajectory: In-house labs Open, Al-augmented innovation ecosystems.
