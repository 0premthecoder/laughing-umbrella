# Tax incidence

• Definition: Tax incidence is the manner in which the burden of a tax is shared among the participants (buyers and sellers) in a market.

• The Surprise Outcome: Regardless of whether the government legally places the tax on the buyer or the seller, the economic burden is shared by both sides. The legal assignment of the tax does not determine who actually pays it; it only changes who physically writes the check to the government.

• Market Effect: Taxation creates a "wedge" between the price consumers pay ($P_c$) and the price producers receive ($P_p$), reducing the overall equilibrium quantity traded in the market.
