# Peter F. Drucker (Father of Modern Management)


Austrian-born consultant and educator who synthesized classical, human relations, and systems theories into a grand unified management discipline.

## Management Theory

- **Management by Objectives (MBO):** Introduced the practice of setting clear, specific, measurable, and time-bound objectives to align employee performance with company vision. This serves as the foundation for modern OKRs (Objectives and Key Results).
    
- **The Knowledge Worker:** Coined the term in the mid-20th century, accurately predicting a future workforce driven by intellectual rather than manual labor. Established that knowledge workers require autonomy, purpose, and self-management.
    
- **Five Operations of Management:** Shifted the focus from managerial activities (Fayol) to outcomes: 1. Setting objectives, 2. Organizing, 3. Motivating and communicating, 4. Measuring performance, 5. Developing people.
    
- **Federalism in Organizations:** Proposed organizing large corporations as federations of semi-autonomous units, combining the resource scale of large enterprises with the agility of small ones.
    
- **Management as a Liberal Art:** Argued that management draws deeply from humanities and social sciences, and emphasized that businesses have a broader social responsibility to their communities beyond mere profit extraction.
