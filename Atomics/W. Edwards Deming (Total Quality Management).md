# W. Edwards Deming (Total Quality Management)

An American mathematical physicist and statistician. While initially under-recognized in the US, he became a foundational figure in post-WWII Japan, where he helped build their economy on the Total Quality Management (TQM) movement. The prestigious Deming Award was established in Japan in his honor.


## Management Theory

[[14 Points of Management ]]


- **PDCA Cycle:** A framework for continuous learning and improvement consisting of four steps: Plan (identify problems/explore solutions), Do (implement on a small/pilot scale), Check (analyze pilot results), and Act (implement fully if successful).

- **Statistical Process Control (SPC):** Deming differentiated between two types of process variations:
    
    - _Systemic/Common causes:_ Inherent to traditional systems; must be addressed by management.
        
    - _Assignable causes:_ Deviations due to specific issues (e.g., material flaws, setup errors); workers must be empowered to address these directly.

- **Psychological Safety:** Deming advocated for driving fear out of the workplace. Employees must feel safe to speak up, take calculated risks, and own mistakes without fear of punishment.

- **Profound Knowledge:** His later work emphasized understanding interdependencies within a system, statistical thinking regarding variation, the epistemology of learning, and human psychology.
