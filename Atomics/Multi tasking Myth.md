# Multi tasking Myth

|**What we believe**|**What research shows**|
|---|---|
|• We do many things at once efficiently.|• We rapidly switch attention; only **~2.5%** can truly multitask ("supertaskers").|
|• Switching is costless.|• Each switch carries a cost: error rates increase, and reduced comprehension.|
|• Productivity rises with parallel tasks.|• Frequent context-switching is correlated with shallower learning and lower meta-cognitive accuracy.|
|• Smartphone alerts boost output.|• Learning: Batch similar tasks and protect blocks for deep work.|
|• Multitasking is a learnable skill.||
