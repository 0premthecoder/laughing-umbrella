# Historical timeline of Business Function

| **Year** | **Milestone**         | **Function**                                                                                                                                     |
| -------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1780     | Industrial Revolution | Production/Operations emerges as the first formal business function. The factory system replaces craft production.                               |
| 1860     | Finance formalizes    | Joint-stock companies, stock markets (NYSE 1792), and limited liability create the need for professional financial management.                   |
| 1900     | Marketing is born     | Mass production requires mass selling. Wanamaker's department stores and advertising agencies emerge in USA. Creating demand becomes a function. |
| 1920     | HR professionalizes   | Post-WWI labor unrest and the Hawthorne Studies formalize 'personnel management'. Workers are not machines.                                      |
| 1950     | Strategy emerges      | Chandler, Ansoff and BCG Consulting separate business strategy from operations as a distinct executive function.                                 |
| 1970     | IT enters business    | Mainframes give rise to MIS. The IBM System/360 turns data processing into a business function.                                                  |
| 1990     | R&D stratifies        | Pharma, tech and consumer goods formalize R&D as central to competitive strategy. IP management rises.                                           |
