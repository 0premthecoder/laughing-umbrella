# Universal Need for Management

Management is required wherever people organize to achieve a common mission, regardless of the entity's size or type:

- **Scope:** Applicable to households, small/medium enterprises (SMEs), large corporations, governments, NGOs, and sports teams.
    
- **Households:** Managing budgets, schedules, and goals (financial or social).
    
- **Small/Medium Enterprises (SMEs):** Viewed as "unbundled" corporations. Functions (marketing, sales, operations, strategy) are often performed by one or few individuals. As the entity grows, these functions become specialized.
    
- **Large Corporations:** Clearly defined functional boundaries (HR, Finance, Operations, Strategic Planning).
    
- **Government/NGOs:** Essential for infrastructure, policy implementation, donor relations, and measuring social impact.
    
- **Analogy:** If an organization is the **body**, management is the **brain**—finding the processes and functions that make the mission achievable.
