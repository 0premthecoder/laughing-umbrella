# Herbert Simon (Decision-Making & Cognitive Psychology)

An American polymath and the only management thinker to win a Nobel Prize in Economics. He also won the Turing Award in computer science, laying foundations for cognitive psychology and artificial intelligence by modeling the human mind as an information processor.

## Management Theory

- **Bounded Rationality:** Simon challenged the classical economic assumption of the "rational, utility-maximizing man." He argued that human rationality is naturally bounded (limited) by imperfect information, restricted cognitive capacity, and time constraints.

- **Satisficing:** A portmanteau of "satisfy" and "suffice." Because decision-makers cannot explore every possible variable to find the absolute optimal solution, they act rationally by accepting solutions that are "good enough" given their constraints.

- **Organizations as Decision-Making Systems:** Simon viewed organizations primarily as mechanisms designed to structure, simplify, and distribute decision-making across their members.

- **Information Processing Model of Cognition**: The mind as symbolic, serial Information processor, This model became the foundation of modern cognitive science and AI

- **The Garbage Can Model:** Co-authored with Cohen and March, this model applies to "organized anarchies" or loosely coupled organizations (e.g., universities, NGOs). It rejects the idea of sequential decision-making. Instead, problems, solutions, participants, and opportunities flow independently and connect opportunistically
