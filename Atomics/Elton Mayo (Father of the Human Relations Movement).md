# Elton Mayo (Father of the Human Relations Movement)

 An Australian psychologist who became a Harvard Business School professor. His work fundamentally shifted management theory toward the social and psychological dimensions of work.

## Management Theory

 - **The Hawthorne Studies (Illinois):** Mayo and his colleagues conducted a series of illumination experiments at the Western Electric plant. They discovered that regardless of whether lighting was increased or decreased, worker productivity rose above the industry average.
    
- **The Hawthorne Effect:** The studies proved that simply being observed, feeling important, and having increased self-esteem motivated workers to reach peak performance. Psychological factors outweighed physical or financial changes.
    
- **The "Social Man":** Countering Taylor's idea of the "Economic Man" (driven purely by financial incentives), Mayo proposed the "Social Man"—workers driven fundamentally by a sense of belongingness, recognition, and interpersonal relationships at work.
    
- **Informal Work Groups:** Mayo highlighted the power of informal groups within the factory. These groups form their own norms, select informal leaders, and use peer pressure (like "binging") to either enforce or resist management goals (e.g., speeding up or slowing down production).
    
- **Participative Supervision:** He recommended that supervisors act more as counselors and facilitators, listening to concerns and involving workers in decision-making, which laid the groundwork for modern employee engagement and collective bargaining.
