# Historical evolution of R&D

|**Historical period**|**Dominant form of the function**|**Major conceptual developments**|
|---|---|---|
|Pre-19th century|Inventor-led innovation and craft experimentation|Individual inventors, workshops, experimentation by artisans and entrepreneurs.|
|Late 19th-early 20th centuries|Industrial research laboratories|Formal corporate labs, applied science, patents and systematic invention.|
|World War II and postwar era|Big science and mission-oriented R&D|Government-funded research, defense science, electronics, aerospace, nuclear technology and university-industry links.|
|1960s-1980s|Product development and portfolio control|R&D budgeting, stage-gate processes, technology forecasting, project selection and product lifecycle management.|
|1990s-2000s|Innovation management and open innovation|Core competencies, absorptive capacity, open innovation, alliances, licensing, user innovation and design thinking.|
|2010s|Agile, lean startup and digital innovation|Rapid prototyping, agile product development, MVPs, platform innovation and data-driven experimentation.|
|2020s-present|AI-assisted discovery and engineering|Generative design, simulation, protein folding, computational chemistry, lab automation, synthetic data and AI research copilots.|
