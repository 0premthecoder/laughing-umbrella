# Elastic & Inelastic

In close substitutes the goods are more elastic 
e.g coke & pepsi, Tea & Coffee, McDonalds & Burger King

When a goods doesn't have alternatives it is less elastic
e.g Eggs, Vegies, Machines, Fuels

Necessities Goods have inelastic demand
e.g Fuel, Electricity, Water and food

Luxuries have elastic Demand
e.g Watches, cars, Necklace

So More substitutes = Very Elastic Demand
called Narrow Market 
Less substitutes = Less Elastic Demand
called Broad Market

e.g Crocin - 500 mg will be in Narrow market but Medicines would be in Broad Market

Demands tends to be more elastic over longer period of time
Like in Short run demand is same but as the time passes people find alternatives 
e.g Petrol Prices high, people switch to public transport or electric vehicles over time
