# Price Ceiling

It is a maximum Price set by Govt on goods and services
like MRP on products

**Non-binding Price Ceiling** :
A **non-binding price ceiling** is a government-imposed maximum price that is set at or above the market equilibrium price. Because it allows the market to naturally clear at its optimal equilibrium, it has no immediate effect on the quantity demanded or supplied.

While a non-binding price ceiling won't change the current market price, it is often implemented as a safeguard against future supply shocks or sudden spikes in demand. 

**Binding Price Ceiling** :
A **binding price ceiling** is a government-imposed legal maximum price set **below** the natural market equilibrium price. Because the ceiling is lower than what the market would naturally clear at, it restricts the price from rising, directly causing the quantity demanded by consumers to exceed the quantity supplied by producers, resulting in a **market shortage**
