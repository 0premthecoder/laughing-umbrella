| **Historical period**              | **Dominant form of the function**            | **Major conceptual developments**                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pre-industrial to 19th century** | Trade, distribution, and merchant selling    | Local exchange, fairs, retailing, early branding, and advertising notices.                        |
| **Late 19th-early 20th centuries** | Mass distribution and advertising            | Driven by railways and newspapers; expansion of national markets and sales forces.                |
| **1900s–1930s**                    | Production and product orientation           | Focus on availability, scale, and efficiency; marketing secondary to manufacturing.               |
| **1930s–1950s**                    | Selling orientation and market research      | Reliance on salesmanship, demand stimulation, and early consumer research due to excess capacity. |
| **1950s–1980s**                    | Marketing concept and segmentation           | Centralization of customer needs, target markets, positioning, and the 4Ps.                       |
| **1990s–2010s**                    | Relationship, service, and digital marketing | Rise of CRM, customer lifetime value, e-commerce, social media, and data-driven channels.         |
| **2020s–present**                  | AI-augmented marketing and growth function   | Reshaped by generative AI, predictive analytics, personalization, CDPs, and privacy awareness.    |
