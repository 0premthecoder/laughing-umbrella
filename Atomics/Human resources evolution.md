# Human resources evolution

Welfare secretaries (1900): The first 'personnel' roles emerges - women welfare workers in UK factories managing basic employees conditions

Hawthorne Studies (1924): Elton mayo: Social factors shape productivity. The human relations movement begins; psychology enters HR

Drucker's insight (1954): The most valuable asset of 21st century institutions will be its knowledge workers. 'HR is reframed as a strategic function'

Strategic HRM (1991): Harvard model - HRM must align with business strategy, HR earns a seat at the strategy table.

The talent war begins (2000): Mckinsey's 'War for Talent' report. Talent acquisition becomes a competitive weapon, especially in technology

HR in the pandemic (2020): Remote work, mental health, DEI and psychological safety. HR's most complex challenge in modern history

Ai-augmented HR (2024): Ai screening, people analytics and chatbot HR support. HR data becomes a strategic intelligent asset
