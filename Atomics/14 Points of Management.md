# 14 Points of Management

- **Create constancy of purpose:** Focus on long-term planning and continuous improvement rather than short-term financial gains.

- **Adopt the new philosophy:** Embrace a culture of quality where mistakes and lethargy are no longer tolerated.

- **Cease dependence on inspection:** Build quality into the process from the beginning rather than relying on end-of-line inspections to catch defects.

- **End awarding business on price alone:** Instead, minimize total cost by building long-term, loyal relationships with single suppliers for each item.

- **Improve constantly and forever:** Continually refine all systems and processes to improve quality and productivity, reducing costs.

- **Institute training on the job:** Provide thorough, modern job training for all employees to ensure consistency and high-level skills.

- **Institute leadership:** Shift management from a focus on numbers and quotas to true leadership, helping people and machines do a better job.

- **Drive out fear:** Encourage open, honest communication so that employees feel safe to ask questions or report problems without fear of retaliation.

- **Break down barriers:** Foster cross-functional collaboration and teamwork across different departments (research, design, sales, production) to achieve common goals.

- **Eliminate slogans and exhortations:** Avoid generic posters or targets asking for "zero defects" or increased productivity; these only create adversarial relationships if workers lack the right systems to succeed.

- **Eliminate numerical quotas:** Replace strict numerical quotas for the workforce and arbitrary numerical goals for management with supportive leadership and process improvement.

- **Remove barriers to pride of workmanship:** Abolish annual merit/rating systems and supervisors' focus solely on sheer numbers, allowing employees to take pride in the quality of their work.

- **Institute education and self-improvement:** Commit to ongoing education and retraining for everyone in the organization to adapt to new technologies and changes.

- **Accomplish the transformation:** Put every single person in the company to work in accomplishing this structural and cultural transformation.
