# Douglas McGregor (Theory X and Theory Y)

A social psychologist and professor at MIT. His influential book, _The Human Side of Enterprise_, explored how managers' assumptions about human nature become self-fulfilling prophecies that dictate organizational culture.


## Management theory

- **Theory X (Traditional/Authoritarian):** Managers assume the average worker dislikes work, avoids responsibility, lacks ambition, and desires only security. Consequently, managers believe workers must be coerced, controlled, micromanaged, and threatened with punishment to achieve goals. This leads to a culture of laziness and resentment.
    
- **Theory Y (Humanistic/Participative):** Managers assume that physical and mental effort in work is as natural as play or rest. They believe workers can be self-directed, seek responsibility, and possess widespread imagination and creativity.
    
- **Goal Integration:** McGregor advocated for Theory Y, emphasizing that management's role is to create conditions where employees can best achieve their own personal goals by directing their efforts toward the success of the organization.
