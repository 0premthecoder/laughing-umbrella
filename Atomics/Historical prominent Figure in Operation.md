# Historical prominent Figure in Operation

Pin Factory (Smith, 1776) : Adam Smith's 'division of labor' multiplies productivity. The
first operations-management insight.

Scientific Management (1911): Frederick Taylor's time-motion studies, standardization,
efficiency engineering. Operations as a science.

Moving Assembly Line (1913): Henry Ford produces the Model T in 93 minutes. The
mass-production paradigm is born.

Toyota Production System (1950): Taiichi Ohno's J-I-T, Kanban, zero-defect philosophy.
Operations becomes a source of competitive advantage.

Six Sigma (1985): Motorola: 3.4 defects per million. Quality management is formalized
as an operations discipline.

Global supply chains (2000): Apple, Nike and IKEA optimize global value chains.
Procurement, logistics and manufacturing globalize.

Supply-chain resilience (2020): COVID-19 disruptions shift the priority from efficiency to
resilience. Near-shoring, reshoring and multi-sourcing rise.
