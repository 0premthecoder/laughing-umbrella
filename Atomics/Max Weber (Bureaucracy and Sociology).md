# Max Weber (Bureaucracy and Sociology)

A founding father of sociology, Weber approached organizations from a broad, historical, and theoretical standpoint.

## Management theory

- **Ideal Bureaucracy** : Weber viewed bureaucracy as a technically efficient organizational form. Its six key features include: 1 fixed jurisdictional areas governed by rules, 2 a clear hierarchy of authority, 3 written documents and manuals, 4 expert training, 5 full-time officials, and 6 management dependent on general rules.

- **Three Types of Legitimate Authority:**

	- _Traditional:_ Based on custom, heredity, and elite social status.
	    
	- _Charismatic:_ Based on the exceptional personal qualities of an individual leader.
	    
	- _Rational-Legal:_ Based on enacted rules and predefined competence. This forms the basis of modern bureaucracy and law.

- **The Protestant Work Ethic:** As a Calvinist, Weber argued that his religion imparted a specific work ethic—devotion to work, frugality (living within means), and the rational accumulation of wealth.

- **Rationalization and the "Iron Cage":** Weber noted that modern society replaced tradition and emotion with highly calculative, reasoning-based action. While liberating, he warned of the "iron cage"—a metaphor describing how rigid organizational rules, roles, and procedures can ultimately strip organizational life of spontaneity, meaning, and innovativeness.
