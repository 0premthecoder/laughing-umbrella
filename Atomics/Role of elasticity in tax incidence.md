# Role of elasticity in tax incidence

The tax burden falls more heavily on the side of the market that is less elastic (more inelastic) because elasticity measures a participant's ability to leave the market when conditions worsen.

• Inelastic Demand & Elastic Supply:

  • Buyers have fewer alternatives or find the good essential (e.g., fuel, medicine).

  • Buyers cannot easily leave the market, so the consumer bears the greater burden of the tax.

• Elastic Demand & Inelastic Supply:

  • Sellers cannot easily change their production or leave the market (e.g., real estate/land).

  • Sellers must accept a much lower price, so the producer bears the greater burden of the tax.
