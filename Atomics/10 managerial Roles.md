# 10 managerial Roles

The 10 Managerial Roles

Interpersonal Roles (Managing through People)

These roles arise directly from a manager's formal authority and involve interacting with people inside and outside the organization:

- **Figurehead:** Performing symbolic and ceremonial duties, such as signing official documents or greeting important guests.
- **Leader:** Motivating, coaching, and guiding employees to ensure the team's performance meets organizational goals.
- **Liaison:** Building and maintaining a network of contacts outside the immediate team to share information and build beneficial relationships.

Informational Roles (Managing by Information)

These roles involve collecting, processing, and disseminating information to keep the organization functioning smoothly: 

- **Monitor:** Actively seeking out relevant information, changes in the industry, and issues within the team.
- **Disseminator:** Transmitting factual or value-based information to subordinates and other members of the organization.
- **Spokesperson:** Communicating official information regarding the organization's performance, policies, and plans to external stakeholders and the public. 

Decisional Roles (Managing through Action)

These roles revolve around processing information and making strategic choices that dictate the direction of the organization:

- **Entrepreneur:** Identifying opportunities, initiating improvements, and launching change within the organization.
- **Disturbance Handler:** Taking corrective action and resolving crises when unexpected problems or conflicts arise.
- **Resource Allocator:** Making critical decisions on who gets what, managing budgets, and assigning time and personnel.
- **Negotiator:** Participating in negotiations and bargaining with other teams or organizations to secure commitments and advance the company's interests.
