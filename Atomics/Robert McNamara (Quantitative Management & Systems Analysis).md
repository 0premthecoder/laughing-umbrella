# Robert McNamara (Quantitative Management & Systems Analysis)

Served as CEO of Ford Motor Company, US Secretary of Defense during the Vietnam War, and President of the World Bank.

## Management Theory

- **Quantitative Revolution:** Formed the "Whiz Kids" team at Ford, using statistical modeling, cost-benefit analysis, and operations research to restore profitability through data-driven decision making.

- **Systems in Government:** Pioneered the Planning, Programming, and Budgeting System (PPBS) to justify expenditures and set smart objectives in public agencies.

- **Systems Analysis**: McNamara institutionalized systems analysis in both corporate and govt management for cost effectiveness, program evaluation and quantitative optimization to complex organizational decisions  

- **Limitations of Metrics:** In his documentary _The Fog of War_, he admitted his failure in the Vietnam War stemmed from relying exclusively on quantitative metrics (e.g., body counts, sorties) while ignoring vital political, cultural, and psychological realities.

- **Core Principle:** Concluded that "not everything that matters can be measured, and not everything measured matters," warning against applying data tools without qualitative wisdom.
