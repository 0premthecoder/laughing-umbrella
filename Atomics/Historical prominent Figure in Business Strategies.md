# Historical prominent Figure in Business Strategies

Sun Tzu (500 BCE): 'The Art of War': know yourself and your enemy. Strategy as
information asymmetry - the first systematic strategic framework.

Scientific management (1911): Taylor: operational strategy - efficiency as competitive weapon. Strategy as internal optimization.

Chandler's thesis (1962): 'Structure follows strategy.' Strategy precedes organization design. Corporate planning is formalized.

Porter's framework (1980): Five Forces, generic strategies, the value chain. Strategy
becomes an academic and consulting discipline.

Resource-based view (1995): Barney and Prahalad - competitive advantage flows from
internal resources and core competencies, not just industry position.

Blue Ocean Strategy (2005): Kim and Mauborgne - create uncontested market space,
combining low cost and differentiation strategies.

Agile strategy (2020): In a VUCA world, OKRs and rolling 90-day strategies and
scenario planning replace 5-year plans. Uncertainty becomes the new constant.
Trajectory: Grand plans Continuous, adaptive strategy under uncertainty.
