# Chester Barnard (Organizations as Social Systems)

A practitioner rather than an academic, Barnard served as the president of New Jersey Bell Telephone for two decades. He authored the seminal book _The Functions of the Executive_.

## Management Theory

- **Cooperative Social Systems:** Barnard defined organizations not just as formal structures, but as cooperative systems requiring coordination between people. An organization only exists as long as its members are willing to cooperate.
    
- **Acceptance Theory of Authority:** Barnard flipped the traditional view of authority. He argued that authority does not flow downward from management; it flows upward from subordinates. A directive only has true authority if the receiver chooses to accept and execute it.
    
- **The Zone of Indifference:** This is the range of orders or directives an employee will follow without questioning. Barnard stated that an executive's job is to widen this zone by using legitimate authority, building trust, and aligning organizational goals with personal values through clear communication.
    
- **Executive Functions:** He identified three core functions for executives: maintaining open communication, securing essential services from individuals through motivation, and defining/formulating the organization's purpose (vision and mission).
    
- **Efficiency vs. Effectiveness:** Barnard clearly distinguished between the two. Effectiveness is about "doing the right things" (achieving goals), while efficiency is about "doing things right" (minimizing waste/burnout). Both are necessary for long-term survival.
