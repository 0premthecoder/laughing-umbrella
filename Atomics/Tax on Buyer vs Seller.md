# Tax on Buyer vs Seller

• Tax on Buyers: Shifts the demand curve to the left (downward) vertically by the exact amount of the tax. Consumers pay a higher price, and producers receive less.

• Tax on Sellers: Shifts the supply curve to the left (upward) vertically by the exact amount of the tax (acting like an increase in input costs). Consumers still pay a higher price, and producers receive less.

• Conclusion: Taxing buyers and taxing sellers are economically equivalent outcomes.

>Tax burden is higher for consumer if Demand is inelastic and higher for supply if demand is very elastic
