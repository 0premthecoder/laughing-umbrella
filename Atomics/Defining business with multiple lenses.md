# Defining business with multiple lenses

| **Management Lens**       | **Definition**                                                                                                                      | **Key Focus**                                                                     | **Example**                       |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Economic**              | A business is a unit that combines resources (land, labor, capital, entrepreneurship) to produce goods and services for the market. | Production, Exchange, Profit, Market efficiency                                   | Reliance Industries               |
| **Value Creation**        | A business exists to create value for customers by solving problems or satisfying needs better than alternatives.                   | Customer needs, Innovation, Competitive advantage, Value proposition              | Apple                             |
| **Organizational**        | A business is a coordinated system of people, processes, technology, and resources working toward organizational objectives.        | Structure, Coordination, Leadership, Decision-making, Human resources             | Amazon                            |
| **Strategic**             | A business is a system that competes within an industry to achieve sustainable competitive advantage.                               | Competition, Industry positioning, Differentiation, Cost leadership, Market power | Walmart<br>(cost effective goods) |
| **Social Responsibility** | A business is a social institution that must balance economic goals with societal welfare.                                          | Stakeholders, Corporate citizenship, ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance)      | Tata Group                        |
| **Entrepreneurial**       | A business is an entrepreneurial venture created to exploit opportunities under uncertainty.                                        | Innovation, Risk-taking, Opportunity recognition, Growth                          | SpaceX                            |
| **Systems**               | A business is an open system interacting continuously with its environment.                                                         | Inputs, Transformations, Outputs, Market feedback                                 | Samsung                           |
| **Digital Platform**      | In the digital economy, many businesses do not merely sell products; they create platforms connecting users.                        | Networks, Data, Ecosystems, Platform effects                                      | Google                            |

from Integrated Management Practice

A business is an organized, value creating economic and social system that mobilizes resources, coordinate activities, serves customers, compete in markets, manage stakeholders and adapts continuously to environmental change in order to sustain growth and achieve organizational objectives
