# Historical evolution of HR

| **Historical period**                   | **Dominant form of the function**                 | **Major conceptual developments**                                                                                                                     |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pre-industrial and early industrial era | Labor control, apprenticeship and welfare         | Master-apprentice systems, paternalism, factory discipline, welfare officers and labor administration.                                                |
| 1900s-1930s                             | Personnel administration and industrial relations | Scientific management, job analysis, time-and-motion studies, wage systems and union relations.                                                       |
| 1930s-1960s                             | Human relations and motivation                    | Hawthorne studies, informal groups, motivation theories, leadership and employee morale.                                                              |
| 1970s-1990s                             | Human resource management (HRM)                   | Strategic HRM, training, performance appraisal, compensation systems, HR planning and culture management.                                             |
| 2000s-2010s                             | Talent, capability and analytics                  | War for talent, competency models, employer branding, HR shared services, HRIS, people analytics and employee engagement.                             |
| 2020s-present                           | AI-augmented workforce function                   | Skills intelligence, workforce analytics, AI-supported recruitment, learning experience platforms, hybrid work and algorithmic management governance. |
